# Atelier Direction

## Evidence of Hands

Viryn Studio should feel inhabited, not manufactured. The interface carries the structure; the art carries the feeling. Neither should compensate for unresolved work in the other.

### The visual test

Every major image begins with one emotional sentence. It is not approved until the composition communicates that sentence without requiring the surrounding copy.

- Gathering: Someone leaves less alone.
- Ledger: Knowledge becomes confident hands.
- Signal: The room is already listening.
- Table: Care arrives before the plate.
- Flagship: An attentive atelier is making places for other people.

### Craft rules

1. One emotional center per image.
2. One symbolic language per room.
3. Light directs feeling, not merely visibility.
4. Paint richly and place sparingly.
5. Private devotional imagery remains private unless every affected person explicitly consents to public use.
6. Symbols must arise from the work: notes, tools, fabric, food, books, cables, brushes, chairs, and other evidence of practice.
7. Geometry may organize a layout. It must not substitute for observation.
8. Buttons share one dependable action grammar even when each framework keeps its own atmosphere.
9. Overlays never compete for the same text space.
10. The final question is: does this feel cared for?
